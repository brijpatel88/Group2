# Team Reflection — Brijesh Patel

## 1. What went well in your team project?

One thing that went well in our team project was teamwork and dividing responsibilities among team members. Since we had multiple people working on different features, everyone focused on a specific part of the project, which helped move development forward faster. We used GitHub branches to separate work and reduce conflicts while making updates.

Communication between team members also helped the project progress. Sometimes there were issues during merging or differences in UI design, but discussing the problems and reviewing updates helped us solve them. We were able to combine different parts into one working application and improve it over time.

Another thing that went well was the final testing and polishing stage. As a team, we reviewed the application, tested features, and made improvements to functionality and design. Seeing separate pieces of work come together into one complete project was a rewarding experience.

## 2. What would you do differently next time?

If I worked on another team project, I would spend more time planning before starting development. In some situations, different coding styles and UI ideas caused extra work later during merging and polishing. Next time, I would recommend setting clearer guidelines from the beginning about coding structure, UI consistency, naming conventions, and responsibilities.

I also think more regular team communication would help. Sometimes people worked on their own parts for a while before syncing changes, which made merging slightly more challenging. Having regular updates or short meetings would help everyone stay aligned and avoid confusion later.

Another improvement would be planning testing earlier instead of waiting until the final stage. Even small changes can affect other sections of an application, so earlier testing would make the process smoother.

## 3. What did you learn about working in a development team?

This project taught me that software development is not only about writing code. Teamwork, communication, and coordination are equally important. Even when different people work on separate tasks, everything eventually needs to connect and function together correctly.

I also learned the importance of version control tools such as GitHub. Before this project, I had limited experience working with branches, merging, and syncing updates from teammates. Through this project, I became more comfortable reviewing changes, resolving small issues, and making sure updates did not break other parts of the project.

Another important lesson was learning patience and flexibility. Team projects are different from individual assignments because everyone works differently. Sometimes adjustments are needed to make sure the final result feels consistent and professional.

## 4. How confident do you feel about working on a real team project now?

I feel more confident compared to before starting this project. Working with teammates, reviewing updates, solving issues, testing functionality, and helping improve the user interface gave me practical experience that feels closer to a real-world software development environment.

Although I still have more to learn, I now feel more comfortable using GitHub, collaborating with others, and contributing to a shared codebase. This experience helped me understand how real development teams work together to solve problems and improve a project over time.
